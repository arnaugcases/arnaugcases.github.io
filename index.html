Car's profile determination

Automatic car washing machines rely on sensors to dermine the distance of the rollers to the surface of the car during each step of the process. In order to optimize it, the objective of this project was to obtain the profile before the washing process, so that the machine's software could compute an optimal path for the roller and thus obtain better results.

To do so, the prototype's design consisted of two towers, one containing laser diodes and the other one photoresistors. Facing the entrance of the prototype, a LiDAR sensor was placed to detect the distance to the toy car. As the toy car moved and blocked laser diodes, the sensors determined the height of the car for each step, thus obtaining in the end its profile.

Hardware

  • PIC18F4520 Microcontroller
  • Laser diodes
  • Photoresistors
  • TF-mini LiDAR
  • Shift registers
  • Operational amplifier
  • Voltage regulator
  • MOSFET
  • Custom PCB

  • C

This project was done in collaboration with Carles Quilis and Antonio Montero.
